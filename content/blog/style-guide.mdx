# What to Wear: Your Session Style Guide

Looking great in photos starts with what you wear. Here are our top recommendations to help you feel confident and look amazing.

## General Tips

- **Solid colors** photograph better than busy patterns
- **Layers** add visual interest and dimension
- **Coordinate, don't match** - aim for complementary colors
- **Avoid logos** and large text on clothing
- **Iron or steam** your clothes beforehand

## Portrait Sessions

### Individual Portraits

For headshots and personal portraits:
- Classic colors: navy, burgundy, emerald, cream
- Well-fitted clothing that makes you feel confident
- Minimal jewelry that won't distract
- Natural makeup that enhances your features

### Family Portraits

- Choose 2-3 complementary colors for the group
- Mix textures (knits, denim, cotton) for visual interest
- Avoid having everyone match exactly
- Consider the location when choosing colors

## Brand & Corporate

For professional headshots and brand photography:
- Stick to your brand colors when appropriate
- Classic, timeless pieces photograph best
- Avoid trendy items that may date quickly
- Bring 2-3 outfit options for variety

## Events

### Formal Events
- Follow the dress code guidelines
- Consider how your outfit will photograph
- Avoid overly reflective fabrics

### Casual Events
- Dress authentically for the occasion
- Comfortable shoes (you'll be on your feet!)

## What to Avoid

- Neon or very bright colors (can cast color onto skin)
- All-black or all-white outfits (can be tricky to expose)
- Very small stripes or patterns (can create moire effect)
- Clothing with visible brand logos

## Preparation Checklist

- [ ] Clothes pressed/steamed
- [ ] Backup outfit packed
- [ ] Comfortable shoes for walking
- [ ] Hair styled as desired
- [ ] Light, natural makeup
- [ ] Get plenty of rest the night before
